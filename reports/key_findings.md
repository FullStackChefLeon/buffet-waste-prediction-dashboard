# Key Findings: Buffet Food Waste Analysis

## 1. Waste Level Distribution

The dataset contains 500 buffet operation records. The waste level distribution shows that most records fall into the Medium waste category, followed by High and Low waste levels.

This indicates that buffet food waste is not limited to a few extreme cases. Instead, moderate waste appears frequently across daily operations, suggesting the need for a structured monitoring and production planning approach.

## 2. Waste by Kitchen Section

Cold Dishes and Cold / Prep showed the highest average leftover ratios:

- Cold Dishes: 0.3575
- Cold / Prep: 0.3484
- Chinese Pastry: 0.2710
- Hot Kitchen: 0.2673

This suggests that cold dishes and preparation-side items may require closer control in production quantity and refill timing. Since these items are often prepared in advance, smaller batch preparation and better end-of-service monitoring may help reduce avoidable leftovers.

## 3. Waste by Meal Period

The average leftover ratios by meal period were:

- Lunch: 0.2930
- Dinner: 0.2928
- Breakfast: 0.2857

Lunch and dinner showed slightly higher leftover ratios than breakfast, but the difference was relatively small. This suggests that buffet waste reduction should not focus only on one meal period. Instead, the kitchen may need a consistent production planning and review process across breakfast, lunch, and dinner services.

## 4. Waste by Event Type

The average leftover ratios by event type were:

- Normal service: 0.3088
- Banquet: 0.2732
- Group tour: 0.2591
- Wedding: 0.2478

Normal buffet service showed the highest average leftover ratio. This may be because normal service has less predictable guest demand compared with banquet, group tour, or wedding events, where guest numbers and service expectations are usually clearer.

This finding suggests that normal buffet service may benefit the most from demand forecasting, leftover monitoring, and refill decision support.

## 5. Operational Implications

Based on the initial analysis, the project should focus on three operational improvements:

1. Identifying high-risk dish categories and kitchen sections.
2. Predicting the waste level of buffet items before or during service.
3. Providing refill recommendations such as full refill, partial refill, or no refill based on demand and service timing.

These findings support the development of a data-driven dashboard for buffet production planning and food waste reduction in hotel kitchen operations.


## 6. Top Dishes by Average Leftover Ratio

The top dishes with the highest average leftover ratios were:

- Fruit Platter: 0.3607
- Cold Cucumber Salad: 0.3548
- Congee Side Toppings: 0.3484
- Braised Pork: 0.2840
- Fried Noodles: 0.2794
- Steamed Fish: 0.2743
- Chinese Pastry Assortment: 0.2713
- Steamed Dumplings: 0.2707
- Stir-fried Vegetables: 0.2647
- Roasted Chicken: 0.2593

The results show that fruit, cold dishes, and side toppings had relatively higher leftover ratios. This supports the earlier finding that Cold Dishes and Cold / Prep sections may require closer monitoring.

From an operational perspective, these items may benefit from smaller batch preparation, more careful end-of-service refill decisions, and weekly review of repeated leftover patterns.
